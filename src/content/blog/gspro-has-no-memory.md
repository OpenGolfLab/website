If you play golf on a simulator, you almost certainly play through [GSPro](https://gsprogolf.com/). It's an outstanding piece of software — gorgeous courses, a great feel, and it works with just about every launch monitor on the market. But it has one blind spot that anyone serious about improving runs into fast:

**GSPro shows you the shot, then forgets it.**

You strike a perfect 7-iron. The carry number flashes on screen — 172 yards, 14° launch, 6,400 spin — and a second later it's gone. There's no record. No history. No way to ask "what does my 7-iron *actually* do across 200 shots?" or "is my driver dispersion tightening or drifting this month?" The simulator is a fantastic present-tense instrument and a non-existent long-term one.

That gap is exactly why we built **[Golf Sim Analytics](/golf-sim-analytics)**.

## Practice without memory isn't really practice

Improvement is a trend, not a moment. The questions that actually move your game are historical:

- What's my *real* carry distance for each club — the median, not the one good one?
- Where do my misses cluster, and is the pattern getting tighter?
- Are my gaps even, or do I have two clubs that go the same distance?
- Is my speed training working over weeks, or am I just having good and bad days?

None of those can be answered from a number that disappears. They require every shot to be captured, stored, and turned into something you can look at over time. GSPro doesn't do that — and honestly, it isn't trying to. It's a simulator, not an analytics platform.

## What Golf Sim Analytics adds

We wrote a free Windows app that sits alongside GSPro and quietly does the part GSPro leaves out:

- **It remembers everything, automatically.** Practice Range exports are picked up the instant they hit your Desktop, and live rounds are tracked in real time and archived when they end. There's no import button — you just play.
- **It turns shots into 15 dashboards.** Dispersion, club gapping, trajectory, swing efficiency, shot shape, speed training, on-course scorecards, live sessions — the historical views GSPro can't give you.
- **It benchmarks you honestly.** Overlay PGA Tour averages or any handicap level from 5 to 20 with one click, so your numbers have a real target.
- **It keeps your data yours.** Everything lives in local Parquet files next to the app. Nothing is uploaded, there's no account, and it's completely free.

Think of it as the layer between "I hit a shot" and "I understand my game."

## Why this is bigger than one app

There's a second reason the memory problem matters. Once every shot is captured in a consistent, structured way, it becomes possible to do something the golf world badly needs: **compare equipment on real data instead of tiny one-off tests.** That's the [OpenGolfLab mission](/lab) — an open, free effort to crowd-source standardized ball and club testing. Golf Sim Analytics is how that data gets captured in the first place.
