You flush a 7-iron. The screen says 172 yards, 14 degrees of launch, 6,400 rpm. It is, by any measure, the best swing you've made all week.

Two seconds later, it's gone.

Not saved. Not logged. Not compared to the 200 other 7-irons you've hit this month. [GSPro](https://gsprogolf.com/) is a brilliant simulator, maybe the best there is, and it has the memory of a goldfish. It renders the shot, shows you the numbers, and forgets both before the ball stops rolling. Every simulator session produces the richest practice data in golf, and nearly all of it evaporates in real time.

That's the problem. Here's the fix, and the much bigger thing the fix makes possible.

## Practice without memory isn't practice. It's exercise.

Improvement is a trend, not a moment. Every question that actually moves your game is historical:

- What does my 7-iron *really* carry: the median of 200 shots, not the one I remember?
- Where do my misses cluster, and is that pattern tightening or drifting?
- Are my gaps even, or do two of my clubs fly the same number?
- Is speed training working over weeks, or am I just having good days and bad days?

A number that vanishes can't answer any of that. A simulator with no memory leaves you making the oldest mistake in golf: judging your game by your best shot instead of your typical one.

## The fix: an app that remembers everything

[Golf Sim Analytics](/golf-sim-analytics) is our free Windows app that sits alongside GSPro and quietly does the part GSPro leaves out.

It captures every shot automatically. Practice Range exports are picked up the instant they land on your Desktop; live rounds are tracked in real time and archived when you finish. There is no import button. You play, and the record builds itself.

Then it turns that record into 15 dashboards: dispersion, gapping, trajectory, swing efficiency, shot quality, speed training, on-course scoring, and more. Overlay PGA Tour averages or any handicap benchmark and see exactly where you stand. The [app page](/golf-sim-analytics) walks through every panel.

And it's local-first. Your shots live in files next to the app, on your machine. No account, no upload, no subscription, no server to leak them. Free, private, yours.

If you've ever wished GSPro could just *remember*, that's the whole pitch. You could stop reading here and get value on day one.

But the memory problem has a second act, and it's the reason OpenGolfLab exists.

## One golfer's memory is practice. A community's memory is knowledge.

Think about how golf equipment gets evaluated today. A robot hits a ball in a lab, a magazine runs a nine-player test on a Tuesday, and a marketing department turns both into a banner ad. None of it tells you what that ball or that driver does for *your* swing speed, *your* handicap, *your* miss.

Now think about what thousands of sim golfers generate every night: launch-monitor-measured shots, all captured the same way, under the same software, with the club and ball attached to every one. That's not a nine-player test. That's the largest standardized equipment study golf has never run, sitting in everyone's basement, evaporating.

So we stopped letting it evaporate. Golf Sim Analytics users can opt in to share anonymized shot data with [the Lab](/lab), and the community dataset does what no individual range session can:

- **Real ball comparisons.** What a premium ball actually buys you in carry and spin, measured across the community, not claimed in an ad.
- **Gapping by handicap.** What a 12-handicap really carries a 7-iron, so you can tell a genuine hole in your bag from a number you read somewhere.
- **Dispersion norms.** How tightly the typical golfer holds each club, so your scatter has honest context.
- **Filters that match you.** Community results cut by club, driver model, iron set, ball, handicap, and age band, because the only comparison that helps your buying decision is one against golfers like you.

That last point is the payoff. Gear costs real money, and the industry's answer to "what should I buy?" has always been "trust us." A community dataset flips that: find golfers with your speed and your handicap, see what their equipment actually does, and spend where the data says the difference is real. Better decisions, backed by measured shots, that show up on your scorecard.

## Built so you can trust it

Crowd data is only as good as its honesty, so the pipeline is deliberately strict, and public:

- **Opt-in and anonymized.** Sharing is off until you turn it on. You pick which sessions go. What ships is stripped to shot metrics plus a display name you choose; raw data never leaves the private store, and only aggregates are ever published.
- **One golfer, one vote.** Every published number is a median of per-golfer medians. Upload 100 sessions or one, you count once, so a marathon uploader can't move a result.
- **Physics-checked.** Every shot passes sanity ranges and per-club plausibility screens before it counts.
- **Open method.** The [entire methodology](/lab/methodology) is versioned and enforced in code anyone can read.
